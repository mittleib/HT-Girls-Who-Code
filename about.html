Girls Who Code is on a mission to close the gender gap in technology and to change the image of what a programmer looks like and does.

Teaching HT girls to code since 2021.

Halfway through her sophomore year, HT student Brynne Mittleider noticed the gender disparity of students in her AP Computer Science A class. CS classes at HT have always been male-dominated, and she realized that this trend needed to change. But, like many across the country, few HT girls have any interest in coding.

Brynne had been a member of a Girls Who Code club at her previous school. The organization helped her learn to collaborate with others and fueled her advancement into recreational coding. She was so passionate about her experience that she wanted to create a similar opportunity at HT. With the help of club sponsor and HT CS teacher, Mrs. Meier, HT's Girls Who Code club was born.

Experience coding as it should be.

At Girls Who Code, we believe that learning to program is more than just analyzing 1's and 0's. We take a well-rounded approach to learning computer languages. Our members learn to code games, websites, algorithms, and more — for fun, or to change the world. Their choice.

Sisterhood Activities

Girls Who Code celebrates Sisterhood, the power of community and passion for activism that unites young women around the world. By playing an informative or just-for-fun game at the start of our meetings, we not only de-stress from the school day, but also build a collective bond that helps reduce fear of failure and boosters collaboration.

Coding Tutorials

Our club offers dozens of tutorials ranging from Beginner to Advanced across multiple languages. Our members learn the concepts of loops, variables, conditionals and functions that form the basis of all programming languages. We work together in a safe and supportive environment of peers to complete projects that solve real-world problems in our community.

Women in Tech Spotlights

We value the female founders of the CS field and learn about their battles against discrimination. With videos and guest speakers, we shine a spotlight on a diverse group of Women in Tech role models to send the message that you can use your skills in technology to uplift your field, your community, and, just as importantly — your own future.

Help degender the computing field.

In 1995, 37% of computer scientists were women. Today, it’s only 22%. The percent will continue to decline if we do nothing. We know that the biggest drop off of girls in computer science is between the ages of 13 and 17.

% of women in Computer Science

Girls Who Code is changing the game. As an international organization, we're reaching girls around the world through our online resources, campaigns, books, and advocacy work. Now, we are on track to close the gender gap in new entry-level tech jobs by 2030.

Don't settle for discrimination.

From learning to code in the classroom to working as a software professional in the CS field, non-male programmers experience rampant discrimination. This adversity is amplified for BIPOC, who are especially underrepresented in STEM. To counter this obstacle, Girls Who Code focuses on maintaining a positive, collaborative environment for learning and project-building. We value:

BRAVERY

We believe being brave is about being resilient, persistent, and ambitious.

SISTERHOOD

We believe that diverse ability, culture, identity, and opinion makes our organization stronger.

ACTIVISM

We’re not just preparing our girls to enter the workforce - we’re preparing them to lead it, to improve it, to completely and totally transform it.
